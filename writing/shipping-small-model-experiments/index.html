Shipping Small-Model Experiments

May 12, 2026 · 6 min read · Placeholder essay

Toy projects often disappear because they were never shaped for reuse. They answer one narrow question, produce a screenshot or a number, and then vanish into a folder named something like final-final-v3. A better outcome is possible without making the project any larger.

Small is not the problem

The failure mode is not scale. It is lack of packaging. A small experiment becomes durable when its inputs, evaluation method, and outcome are easy to identify. Once that happens, the project can be revised instead of restarted.

Give every experiment an exit path

Before starting, it helps to decide what the finished artifact could be. For this site, there are a few acceptable endings:

  • A project card with one concrete lesson.
  • A short writing entry describing the setup and the result.
  • A reusable repo skeleton that can support the next iteration.
Practical default.

If an experiment does not earn a full writeup, it should at least leave behind one paragraph explaining what was tested and what decision it informed.

Document what made the result trustworthy

Trust does not come from length. It comes from specific details: which baseline was used, what changed, how often the result held, and what would make the conclusion collapse.

The site structure here is designed around that pattern. Project pages hold the durable summary. Writing pages hold the reasoning. Together they keep small work from becoming throwaway work.
