A public notebook for learning by building.

This page is still a draft, but the intended shape is clear: a place to collect experiments, keep honest notes about what worked, and slowly turn curiosity into a better technical practice.

What this site is for

James Z is using this site less like a polished personal brand page and more like a durable operating log. The goal is to document projects clearly enough that a future version of the author can understand both the outcomes and the reasoning that produced them.

In the near term, that means placeholder text, draft project summaries, and sample writing. Over time, those placeholders should be replaced with concrete case studies, better diagrams, and honest retrospectives.

Working principles

Make experiments legible

Write enough context around each run that the result can be understood later without reconstructing the entire environment from memory.

Prefer small proofs first

Before scaling a workflow or claiming novelty, prove that the basic setup is sound and that the baseline is actually understood.

Publish the process, not just the wins

Dead ends, ablations, and revisions are often more useful than polished final screenshots. This site should leave room for that.
