## Why this paper?



I chose to read this paper to better prepare myself for my upcoming research meetings.



## Problem Statement



There are sapling students and pine students. The goal is to strive to be a pine student that is able to grow whereas a sapling student will feel stuck and not be able to grow as a researcher.



## Take aways to become a pine student



1. Arrive to mentor meetings with an agenda of all the points to discuss. You can collaborate with another students to come up with the agenda. Go through the list and prioritize the most pressing issues. This allows the student to think about the bigger picture. 

2. Don't put off weekly meetings and be proactive by messaging the mentor about any potential roadblocks or relevant work. Communication is key!

3. Establish the research question and what the goals are at the beginning of the meeting to set the context for the rest of the meeting.

4. When generating data and bringing it to a meeting, be sure to put in time to review the data yourself and ask if there's anything useful. Make sure to look at random parts of the data, not just the first few lines. If something is unusual, the error should be corrected and regenerate the results. 

5. When collecting data, start off with a smaller sample of the dataset(first 1000~ examples) to detect early bugs. Getting small results quickly is important for building intuition on research questions. Attempt to estimate how long it would take to run the program.

6. When plotting data, make sure to have labels on axes and other interpretable visualizations, including proper scaling. Also, it is always nice to have your code readily available if you need to regenerate data.

7. During meetings, make sure to take notes in multiple formats and ask for clarification questions if there's any confusion. For example, it's helpful to restate what you think is happening and then ask if that's right. It is helpful to record audio as well if needed; this allows for active participation. Be sure to also take any pictures of diagrams and upload them for everyone to reference.

8. At the end of the meeting, make an effort to suggest what you think the next steps are and why. For example, you can talk about how to address the current roadblock to move onto the next milestone. It is also possible to challenge (but this can get exhausting real quick, so do it with moderation) the mentor's next steps and suggest new directions for the project as a whole.



## Looking Forward

I look forward to applying these tips to my upcoming meetings and become a stronger researcher.
